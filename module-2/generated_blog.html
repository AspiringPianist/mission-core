Understanding Signal Coupling and Measurement

Understanding signal coupling and measurement is crucial in the context of an oscilloscope, which is an essential tool for analyzing electrical signals. GND coupling, DC coupling, and AC coupling are fundamental concepts related to how an oscilloscope connects to a signal source. GND coupling refers to grounding the oscilloscope to the signal source to eliminate noise. DC coupling allows the oscilloscope to measure both DC and AC components of a signal, while AC coupling blocks the DC component and only measures the AC component. RMS (Root Mean Square), average, and peak-to-peak voltage are essential parameters of a signal. RMS voltage gives the equivalent DC voltage that would deliver the same power to a resistive load, average voltage is the mean voltage over a cycle, and peak-to-peak voltage is the difference between the highest and lowest voltages in a signal cycle. These parameters can be measured and displayed on an oscilloscope.
